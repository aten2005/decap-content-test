# Soft launch vs hard launch: when and how to post your partner
    
    So you've found someone worth keeping around, and now you're facing the millennial dilemma: when do you make it Instagram official? Let's break down your options.
    
    ## What's a Soft Launch?
    
    The soft launch is your relationship's teaser trailer. Think: two coffee cups, a mysterious hand in frame, or your partner's back facing the camera during a sunset. You're telling the world "I'm taken" without revealing who's doing the taking. It's perfect for the early stages when you're vibing but not ready for your aunt's "when's the wedding??" comments.
<br/>
    **Common moves:** Cropped photos, strategically blurred faces, "someone took me here üòä" captions, or the classic mirror selfie where they're conveniently cut out.

    ## When to Soft Launch
    
    **Weeks 1-8:** If you post at all, keep it whisper-soft. Stories only, very ambiguous. You're still figuring out if they load the dishwasher correctly.
<br/>
    **Months 2-4:** Prime soft launch territory. You're confident this is going somewhere, but not ready for public pressure. Drop those artistic shoulder shots and let your followers play detective.
    
    ## When to Hard Launch
    
    Wait until you can check these boxes:
    <br/>
    - You've had the exclusivity talk (crucial!)
    - You've met each other's friends
    - You're not worried about jinxing it
    - **They're comfortable being posted** (get consent!)
    - You can handle family members asking about marriage in the comments
    - You're past the "discovering dealbreakers" phase
    
    ## The Real Talk
    
    Here's the truth: your relationship's validity isn't determined by Instagram. Some people hard launch after three weeks and thrive. Others stay soft launch forever. Some never post at all.
<br/>
    **The only rules that matter:**
<br/>
    1. Both people should be comfortable with what you post
    2. Don't perform your relationship for likes
    3. Post when it feels right, not because you feel pressured
